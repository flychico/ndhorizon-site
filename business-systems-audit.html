Common questions

Business Systems Audit FAQ

What do you need from me?

Answers to 16 questions: services, target customer, lead sources, follow-up process, tools, team size, average job value, your three nearest competitors, and goals. About 20 minutes. No logins, financials, or sensitive documents required.

What is the mystery shop?

With your written consent on the intake form, we contact your business the way a real lead would (your website form or phone) and time what happens. The result appears in your report with timestamps. It is consistently the finding owners act on first, because it is proof rather than opinion.

What happens on the findings call?

Thirty minutes, within a few days of delivery. We walk through your scorecard, explain the reasoning behind each finding, answer questions, and agree what to tackle first. No sales pitch is required for implementation; the report speaks for itself.

How is this different from a free consultation?

A consultation is a conversation. The audit is a complete written analysis of your business with a scorecard, prioritized issues, and a 30-day plan you keep. It is the difference between talking about your business and holding a map of it.

Does the audit include implementation?

No. The audit and findings call tell you exactly what to fix and in what order, written so you can execute it yourself. If you want it built for you, the Operations and Growth tracks above are scoped directly from your report, which doubles as the project plan.

What kinds of businesses does this work for?

Service-based businesses: healthcare and recovery, transportation, security, document preparation, professional services, and local service businesses. If your business runs on inquiries, appointments, and relationships, the audit fits.

Do you handle sensitive information?

No. The audit works from your answers and public information. For healthcare businesses, we never request or process patient information. We audit your processes, not your records.
