You already know the symptoms. An inquiry comes in while you are with a customer and never gets a call back. A quote goes out and sits there because the follow-up was not tracked. Common questions

Business Systems Audit FAQ

What do you need from me?

Answers to 16 questions: services, target customer, lead sources, follow-up process, tools, team size, average job value, competitors, and goals. About 20 minutes. No logins, financials, or sensitive documents required.

What is the contact-channel test?

With your written consent on the intake form, we contact your business the way a real lead would and review what happens. The result appears in your report with timestamps.

What happens on the findings call?

Thirty minutes. We walk through your scorecard, explain the reasoning behind each finding, answer questions, and agree what to tackle first. No sales pitch is required for implementation.

How is this different from a free consultation?

A consultation is a conversation. The audit is a complete written analysis of your business with a scorecard, prioritized issues, and a 30-day plan you keep.

Does the audit include implementation?

No. The audit and findings call tell you exactly what to fix and in what order, written so you can execute it yourself. If you want it built for you, implementation is scoped directly from your report.

What kinds of businesses does this work for?

Service-based businesses: healthcare and recovery, transportation, security, document preparation, professional services, and local service businesses. If your business runs on inquiries, appointments, and relationships, the audit fits.

Do you handle sensitive information?

No. The audit works from your answers and public information. For healthcare businesses, we never request or process patient information. We audit your processes, not your records.
